Key terms (Terms You should Know)

Cookie
Small files of information of that a web server generates and sends to a web browser. Cookies help inform websites about the user, enabling the websites to personalize the user experience. Source: https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/privacy/what-are-cookies/
Third party cookie
Cookie that belongs to a domain other than the one displayed in the browser. Third-party cookies are most often used for tracking purposes. Source: https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/privacy/what-are-cookies/
Hypertext transfer protocol secure(HTTPS)
The secure version of HTTP, and primary protocol used to send data between a web browser and a website.Source:https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/ssl/what-is-https/
Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP)
The foundation of the World Wide Web, and is used to load webpages using hypertext links. HTTP is an application layer protocol designed to transfer information between networked devices and runs on top of other layers of the network protocol stack, however not secure.
Source:https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/ddos/glossary/hypertext-transfer-protocol-http/
Do Not Track Setting
Browser setting that sends a ‘Do not track’ request to websites when a user visits the website through requesting not to store cookies during searching the imternet. However enabling this setting is not effective on its own. Source: https://www.cookieyes.com/knowledge-base/cookies-101/what-is-do-not-track/#:~:text=Do%20Not%20Track%20is%20a,stop%20websites%20from%20tracking%20users.
High entropy hint
Unique, and specify information that identifies the browser, and the user. Examples include: Font, operating system version, specific device model name, etc.
Low entropy hint
General information about a browser, and does not uniquely identify the user or browser. For example, a low entropy hint is value that a browser is on a mobile device and does not state which device mobile.

Accessibility features

Here are some tips and sugguestion to protect your privacy!

  1. Enable safe Browsing settings!

    2. This feature will detect phishing websites and block popups.

    Steps for Google Chrome: Go to Settings > Privacy and Security > Security > Click button by “Standard protection” to be protected against dangerous websites, downloads, and extensions

    For Microsoft Edge: Go to Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Security > Turn on Microsoft Defender SmartScreen and Block potentially unwanted apps.

  2. Keep browser up-to-date and make a routine to install updates!

    3. Steps for Google Chrome: Click the three vertical dots in the top-right corner > Go to Help > About Google Chrome. Chrome will automatically check for and download any available updates. If an update is available, click > Relaunch 

  3. Disable third-party cookies!

    4. For Google Chrome: Go to Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data > Click button by “Block third-party cookies.” Edge: Go to Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and data stored > Manage and delete cookies and site data > Turn on “Block third-party cookies."

    Source: https://utiasecurity.tennessee.edu/recommended-secure-browser-settings/
  4. Block pop-ups

    5. Chrome: Go to Settings > Privacy and security > Site Settings > Content > Pop-ups and redirects and click button by “Don’t allow sites to send pop-ups or use redirects.” Edge: Go to Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Pop-ups and redirects > Turn on Block.

  5. Restrict site permissions as much as possible!

    6. Deny access to your location, camera, or microphone unless these permissions are required for the website to function properly.Source: https://www.cisa.gov/resources-tools/training/tips-stay-safe-while-surfing-web-part-1-web-browser-settings
